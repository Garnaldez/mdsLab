Lola received her B.Sc. in Pharmacy from Universidad Complutense de Madrid (UCM, 2004), and later pursued a Master’s degree in Research and Development of New Drugs at Universidad de Navarra (2007). For her PhD, she joined the lab of Manuela G. López at Universidad Autónoma de Madrid (UAM), where she studied the role of the master antioxidant regulator NRF2 in major depression. Here, she also contributed to the repurposing of known drugs for neurodegenerative disorders such as Alzheimer’s disease. Additionally, she enjoyed two scientific stays at University College London (UK) and Universidad de Navarra.

In 2012, she started her post-doctoral research in the lab of Dr. Peter Penzes at Northwestern University (Chicago, USA). Her work primarily centered on studying the role of synaptic proteins in neuropsychiatric disorders, with a specific focus on the regulation of CNTNAP2 by ectodomain shedding. She explored how this regulatory mechanism impacts calcium dynamics and neuronal connectivity.

In September 2020, she secured an Assistant Professor position at UCM and joined the Neurotransmission and Neurorreparation Group. Briefly after, she was awarded with a grant from the Spanish Research Agency to establish her independent research program. This program aims to investigate whether ectodomain shedding regulates neurodevelopmental conditions such as autism, schizophrenia, and epilepsy.

In addition to her research, she teaches Clinical Biochemistry, Biochemistry and Molecular Biology to Pharmacy and Biochemistry undergraduates at Universidad Complutense.

In her free time, she loves travelling, running, and enjoying some quality time with her three kids and her partner.

Miguel received his B. Sc. in Chemistry from Universidad Autónoma de Madrid and completed his Master’s in Biochemistry, Molecular Biology and Biomedicine at Universidad Complutense. He first joined the lab for his Master’s project in 2022 and stayed to complete his Thesis. His thesis research investigates the different determinants of the molecular and functional composition of the brain sheddome. He is also interested in deepening into the understanding of how the regulation of risk factors for neuropsychiatric disorders by ectodomain shedding impacts neuronal function. In his free time, he officiates soccer matches, making split-second decisions that can turn the tide of the game!

Silvia obtained her B. Sc. in Biochemistry from Universidad Autónoma de Madrid. She joined the Sheddome and Disease lab in September 2023 to do her research project as part of the Master’s in Neurosciences at Universidad Complutense. Her research focuses on how neuronal activity and neuroplasticity shape ectodomain shedding. When she is not in the lab, she enjoys going to concerts, particularly if they are in Lanzarote, her island of origin.
